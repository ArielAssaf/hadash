Laws Passed

Laws we successfully passed for the benefit of the public

work

Workers' Rights

expand_more

Prohibition on taking security guarantees from employees

By law, employers who take security guarantees, checks, bills, or deposits from their employees will be subject to imprisonment and fines. The law also applies to contractor workers.

Empowering the Labor Court to extend deadlines

The purpose of the law is to allow the Labor Court to extend deadlines for filing lawsuits against the National Insurance Institute and appeals on its decisions. Until now, the law did not allow the court to extend these deadlines, even if the reasons were justified.

Labor Law Enforcement Law

Changing the law to include real mechanisms for protecting contractor workers. Currently only in cleaning, security, and catering sectors. An evolution of the bill "Client Responsibility for Contractor Workers' Rights" – which established that responsibility for maintaining the rights of contractor workers rests not only on the contractor employing them but also on the client ordering the services. The bill was prepared for second and third readings in the previous Knesset and led to the government bill that was finally accepted.

Sick Pay Law (Absence due to child's illness)

Grants parents of children with disabilities the right to additional vacation days and absence hours to assist their children. Promoted together with MK Uri Maklev.

Notification to Employee Law

An amendment ensuring contractor workers receive information on the contract terms under which they are employed. This law aims to correct a situation where the worker was not told what conditions were agreed upon between the contractor and the actual employer, and ensure that upon commencement of employment, the worker is given the employment terms to which the contractor is committed vis-à-vis the actual employer. The law requires that the worker be given the necessary information to ensure the realization of their rights. Together with MK Haim Oron.

Tenders Law Amendment (Prohibition of age discrimination)

The law aims to prevent a situation where a person is discriminated against in tender proceedings due to their age. Usually, this refers to discrimination against the older population, which is in a "risk group" for unequal and discriminatory treatment due to relatively advanced age compared to younger competitors in the tender.

Paid Sabbatical on Local Election Day

Our private bill accelerated a government bill and was merged with it into law.

Prevention of Cyclical Dismissals

Prevents the employment of workers on fixed-term contracts and cyclical dismissals followed by re-employment – a method used by employers to evade the obligation of social benefits and severance pay.

Human and Social Rights

expand_more

Prohibition of discrimination based on age

Prohibition of discrimination based on age in the supply of a product or public service, in granting entry to a public place, or in providing service in a public place, determining that discrimination against a patient on grounds of age is a finable offense and also a civil tort. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK Orit Strock.

Prohibition of charging entrance fees to public parks

Amendment to the Municipalities Ordinance prohibiting local authorities from charging entrance fees to public parks.

Prohibition of garnishing accompaniment allowance for the blind

Accompaniment allowances, enabling blind people to pay for help required for basic mobility (e.g., shopping at the supermarket) and social integration, will now be protected from all types of garnishment. Since a significant portion of the blind population in Israel lives below the poverty line, many suffer from garnishments on their accounts due to debts.

Abolition of imprisonment for debtors in Execution Office

This law abolishes imprisonment as a means of collecting debts in the Execution Office (Hotzaa LaPoal), except for imprisonment for alimony debt. Alongside this, the law increases the collection means available to the creditor. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK Merav Michaeli.

Restrictions on carrying weapons and obtaining a firearm license

This law considers not only the medical fitness of the firearm license applicant but also their mental fitness, and conditions the renewal of a firearm license on a psychologist's confirmation of the applicant's mental fitness. Additionally, the proposal establishes that the applicant must not have a criminal record in the offenses listed, concerning violence, ranging from assaulting a public servant to rape and murder. Promoted together with a group of MKs led by MK David Tzur.

The Law for Establishing a Fund from Gas Profits for Social Goals

The complementary side to the stubborn struggle we led for the public's share of gas revenues and natural resources in general. This law ensures that gas revenues will not be swallowed into the general hole of the state budget but will be truly invested in social goals.

Eligibility for compensation for Holocaust survivors who immigrated to Israel after 1953

The law corrects the injustice caused to date to Holocaust survivors, canceling the discrimination regarding those who immigrated after 1953. A private bill we promoted through the Lobby for Holocaust Survivors, of which Dov Khenin is a co-chair, was adopted by the government and became law.

Right to presence of a companion during medical treatment

The law anchors this right in legislation so that the default will be that the patient has the right to a companion present during medical treatment, thereby assisting the patient and in quite a few cases making the language and medical service received more accessible. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK Pnina Tamano-Shata.

Holocaust Survivors Benefits Law

Expands benefits given to Holocaust survivors from concentration camps and ghettos. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK Haim Katz.

Entry into Israel Law (Extension of license for foreign worker in nursing)

Allows, in certain circumstances, continued stay in Israel for nursing caregivers at the end of their license period.

Law transferring funds and property of unlocated Holocaust victims to assist needy Holocaust survivors

Promoted together with MK Merav Michaeli.

Textbook Lending Law

The amendment stipulates that every school must operate a textbook lending program. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK Amram Mitzna.

Crime Victims' Rights Law

Anchors the status of victims of hit-and-run offenses in discussions on plea bargains with offenders.

Planning and Building Budget Transparency and Audit Law

The purpose of the law is to increase the observance of the principle of legality, proper administration, integrity, efficiency, and savings in the operation of local planning and building committees and to increase the transparency of their actions. The first part of the law established that an internal auditor shall be appointed for every local planning and building committee. The second part of the law established that a summary of the approved budget of local committees shall be published both in the official gazette and on websites. This is to increase transparency in the work of local committees and bring information on the financial activity conducted in them to the public's attention.

Prevention of Criminal Record for Military Offenses Law

Such as refusing an order or absence from service. In December 2010, this proposal passed together with a similar proposal by MK Miri Regev in a preliminary reading and was transferred for discussion in the Foreign Affairs and Defense Committee.

Promotion of Competition in the Food Industry Law

Among other things, by preventing geographic concentration and increasing regional competition, and by imposing a duty on large retailers to publish online the prices of products sold in each of their stores, enabling current ongoing comparison of basket prices. Promoted together with a group of MKs led by MK Nachman Shai.

Appropriate Prison Conditions Law For Prisoners

The bill ensures prison conditions that maintain dignity and health, medical treatment, required supervision, a bed, a mattress, blankets, a closet, suitable and healthy food, ventilation and light in the cell, and a daily walk in the open air. The law applies to all prisoners without distinction, including security prisoners.

Separate tax calculation for couples in a joint business

The law replaces the existing arrangement stating that separate tax calculation for spouses can only be requested in situations where there is no dependence between their income sources, in a way that allows separate tax calculation for spouses where there is dependence between their income sources. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK Moshe Gafni.

Legal representation in hearings regarding holding prisoners in solitary confinement

The obligation of representation, now anchored in law, will contribute to reducing solitary confinement to truly essential situations.

Providing a human telephone answer within 3 minutes

Law requiring companies operating call centers to provide a human answer within 3 minutes from the moment of connection. Promoted with a group of MKs led by Eitan Cabel.

Reducing the obligation to wear a helmet when riding bicycles

The law significantly scales back the law from the 17th Knesset, which established that riding a bicycle without a helmet is a criminal offense. According to the law, the obligation will be limited only to extreme and sports riding outside the city and for children and youth.

Patients' Rights Law Amendment

Adding a registered nurse to the ethics committee established under the Patients' Rights Law. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK Shuli Moalem-Refaeli.

Nazi Persecution Disabled Persons Law Amendment

Expands benefits given to these disabled persons. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK David Tzur.

Traffic Ordinance Amendment (Duty to provide information regarding vehicle pledges)

Determines that upon transfer of vehicle ownership, information regarding the existence of a pledge on the vehicle will be automatically checked.

Women's Rights

expand_more

Income support benefit for women in shelters for battered women

Staying in a shelter for battered women cannot serve as an alibi for the state to shirk responsibility. Women staying in shelters for battered women need income support more than anything since they have no other source of livelihood and arrive at the shelter destitute.

Ensuring alimony payment even for a woman who visited abroad

The new law states that National Insurance alimony payments cannot be withheld from women traveling abroad.

Increasing compensation for sexual harassment

Promoted together with a group of MKs led by MK Michal Rozin.

Prohibition of Advertising Prostitution Services Law

Dealing with the economic mechanism underlying the prostitution industry.

Prevention of Raising Retirement Age for Women Law

The law cancels the automatic raising of the retirement age as previously established by law.

Adjustment grant for women leaving a shelter for battered women

Currently, women who leave a shelter where they stayed due to fear for their lives and their children's lives cannot return to their homes, so the bill will grant them the right to an adjustment grant for them and their children. This bill joins another bill of mine and MK Galon which passed second and third readings, allowing a woman in a shelter for battered women to receive income support.

Reserving places for women in lists for local authorities

Another step to dismantle entry barriers preventing women from integrating into leading positions in local government and politics in general. The law was promoted in cooperation with MKs Yifat Kariv, Aliza Lavie, and Hanin Zoabi.

Equal Pay for Male and Female Employees Law Amendment

Imposing an obligation on public employers to collect and publish wage data by gender as a way to deal with existing gender wage gaps. Promoted with a group of MKs led by MK Aliza Lavie.

Prevention of Sexual Harassment Law Amendment

Amendment defining an offense of sexual harassment by a public official, even when there is no active objection by the harassed person due to hierarchy, dependence, and authority in the situation. Promoted together with MK Michal Rozin.

Child Protection

expand_more

Prohibition on selling cigarettes via vending machines

The law aims to reduce the accessibility of cigarettes and smoking products for youth.

Prohibition of Selling Lottery Tickets to Minors Law

The law aims to prevent the sale of lottery tickets to those under 18 years of age, to protect minors from gambling addiction and waste of large amounts of money.

Law canceling discrimination against Haredi orphans

Law balancing the rights of Haredi orphans with other orphans and allowing them to receive survivors' benefits from National Insurance until age 20. Promoted together with MK Nissim Zeev.

Local Authorities Law (Earmarking educational allocation funds)

The law prohibits garnishing local authority funds designated for education.

Law to extend the activity of the Fund for Children and Youth at Risk

The Fund for Children and Youth at Risk was originally established as a means to deal with the expansion of poverty, distress, and social problems among children and youth with the large cut in child allowances. Over the years, the Fund has developed a variety of assistance activities for at-risk children and youth, and its activity is praised from all sides. The Fund was initially established under a temporary order only planned to expire at the end of 2007. The law extended the Fund's activity by a year.

Information Delivery to Parent Law

Anchors the right of two parents living separately to receive information regarding their children.

Youth Law Amendment (Trial, Punishment, and Modes of Treatment) – (Alternative Proceedings)

Instead of sending teenagers to prison and training future criminals – the law creates a system of alternative proceedings, in which they take responsibility for their actions and are involved in corrective actions in various circles. Global experience teaches that this is fairer and also more effective.

Environment and Health

expand_more

Clean Air Law

This is a law approved for first reading in the 16th Knesset and re-formulated in the 17th Knesset with the active involvement of MK Khenin. This is a comprehensive law regulating, for the first time in Israel, the issue of pollutant emissions into the air comprehensively, while tightening the ring around polluting industry.

Prohibition of Driving on Beaches Law

The law sets clear prohibitions and definitions for driving on beaches, sharpens punishment, and creates effective and modern enforcement mechanisms for the prohibition of driving on beaches.

Environmental Enforcement Law

Grants inspectors of the Ministry of Environmental Protection broad search and investigation powers. For the first time, subordinates various security systems to civil enforcement.

Environmental Enforcement Law (Local Authorities)

The proposal grants local authorities enforcement powers for environmental laws, thereby achieving simultaneously an important environmental goal (increasing enforcement of environmental laws) and an important social goal (flowing fine money from environmental polluters directly to the coffers of the authorities whose residents are the main victims of the pollution).

Protection of the Kinneret Law

A law applying the provisions of the Protection of the Coastal Environment Law to the shores of the Kinneret as well, and establishing an authority (Kinneret Cities Association) that will act to protect the Kinneret and its shores.

Protection of Eilat Gulf Coastal Environment Law

The law applies for the first time the Protection of the Coastal Environment Law, applicable to the Mediterranean shores, also to the Gulf of Eilat.

The Polluter Pays Law

A cross-cutting law updating and correcting all environmental laws in Israel. The law includes advanced mechanisms of economic punishment – administrative and criminal – against polluters. The law stiffens punishment for entities polluting without a license and establishes a collection mechanism also from entities polluting in accordance with the law. This mechanism is supposed to incentivize factories to switch to cleaner technologies.

The New Deposit Law

The law imposes direct responsibility on manufacturers based on the "polluter pays" principle.

National Mine Action Authority Law

The law establishes an authority to act for the clearance and removal of hundreds of minefields scattered across the country.

Environmental Transparency Law – Duty to report on pollutant emissions

A law requiring transparency regarding emissions of pollutants into the environment, imposing detailed reporting duties on factories, establishing a registry of emitted pollutants, and requiring publication of data for public knowledge. Experience teaches that environmental reporting duties change the behavior of polluting factories. Environmental transparency is a democratic tool and a central instrument for mobilizing the public to act against pollution and polluters. Evasion of reporting will be a criminal offense and will also entail significant administrative financial sanctions.

Energy Efficiency in Public Buildings Law

Establishes a comprehensive mechanism for reducing energy usage in the public system. Among other things, every public institution is committed to a defined plan for reducing energy consumption. It also includes a series of measures for energy saving including installation of energy-saving bulbs, replacement of electrical equipment, and procedure for its economical operation.

Mandatory Installation of Water Saving Devices in Public Buildings Law

Requires installation of water-saving devices, including flow restrictors, in all public buildings to save water.

Law for Reducing Air Pollution from Transportation (Amendment 84 to Traffic Ordinance)

The law creates a clear arrangement in the division of powers between the government and local government and ends the difficult situation where each of the two sides could paralyze the other's initiatives. The amendment requires the local authority to act to reduce air pollution from transportation in any situation where there is a high level of air pollution.

Electronic Waste Recycling Law

Electronic waste can cause soil and groundwater pollution. At the center of the law is the important social principle of "polluter pays". The law expands the principle of producer responsibility for treating such waste and reducing it, and establishes a mechanism for regulating the collection and recycling of purchased devices.

Arnona Exemption for Field Schools Law

The law assists environmental educational institutions.

Animal Protection

expand_more

Prohibition on Importing Cosmetics and Cleaning Products Tested on Animals Law

The law prohibits the import into Israel of cosmetics and cleaning products, not for health purposes, whose production process involved animal testing.

Prohibition on Declawing Cats Not for Medical Purposes Law

The declawing prohibited by the law is usually performed due to the desire of cat owners to protect home furniture. It is an irreversible surgery causing great pain to the animal.

Dog Neutering/Spaying Law

The purpose of the law is to deal humanely with the phenomenon of thousands of stray dogs euthanized annually in agony or experiencing severe abuse. According to the law, anyone who has their dog neutered or spayed will recoup the investment within two years thanks to the discount they will receive on the license fee.

Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Law Amendment (New tools for law enforcement)

The new law allows government inspectors to enter any place (except a residence) to perform inspections and verify that the provisions of the law are being met. The law adds authority to demand documents, take measurements, take samples, and more. Supervision and enforcement powers formulated two decades ago have been replaced by modern provisions suitable for the twenty-first century. Improvements were also made to the provision allowing animal protection organizations to request injunctions against abuse. "Let the Animals Live" accompanied the bill, and it was promoted together with MK Eitan Cabel.

Democracy and Transparency

expand_more

Applying Freedom of Information Law and Transparency Duties to Sports Associations

Promoted together with MK Yariv Levin.

Transparency in Land Authority Decisions

Requires the Land Authority, which controls more than 90% of state lands, to publish all its decisions immediately on the Internet. This is to prevent the possibility of corruption, discrimination, and preference for cronies and tycoons. Before the legislation, only 51 out of 1800 management decisions of the Authority were published.
