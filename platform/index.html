Hadash's Political and Social Program

The Democratic Front for Peace and Equality

handshake

A. A Just, Comprehensive, and Stable Israeli-Palestinian and Arab-Israeli Peace

expand_more

A. Ending the occupation of all territories occupied since 1967 and onwards (Palestinian, Syrian, and Lebanese territories) and achieving a permanent solution to the Palestinian issue. This is in order to reach a state of peace and stability in the country and the region, based on respect for the rights of peoples.

The solution to the Palestinian issue is based on:

  • Complete withdrawal from all territories occupied in 1967.
  • Evacuation of all settlements in the West Bank, including occupied East Jerusalem.
  • Establishment of a fully sovereign Palestinian state with East Jerusalem as its capital, alongside the State of Israel with West Jerusalem as its capital.
  • A solution to the issue of Palestinian refugees from their homeland based on the right of return and/or compensation, in accordance with UN resolutions.

To advance towards a solution to the Palestinian issue, Hadash struggles against the occupation:

  • To stop all settlement activity in the West Bank and East Jerusalem, including the robbery and looting of private Palestinian lands, including those that the occupation authorities term "state lands." Ending the crimes of Palestinian home demolitions and removing all measures and restrictions designed to prevent Palestinians from building their homes on their lands.
  • To stop all settlement and oppressive practices in occupied East Jerusalem, including attacks on holy sites, and restrictions on the lives of Palestinians in the city of Jerusalem.
  • Ending the siege imposed on the Gaza Strip, ensuring direct connection between Gaza and the West Bank, and opening Gaza's borders to the world.
  • Removing checkpoints in the West Bank and ensuring freedom of movement for Palestinians in the West Bank, including access to their jobs and agricultural lands.
  • To stop all settler attacks carried out under the auspices and protection of the Israeli army against Palestinians and their property in the West Bank, including occupied East Jerusalem.
  • Release of political prisoners, ending the use of administrative detention, and ending all forms of physical and psychological torture. Likewise, ending collective punishments imposed against Palestinians.

B. Protection of Workers' Rights and for Social Justice

expand_more
  1. Hadash fights against the official economic policy that completely contradicts the principle of social justice - both in the distribution of resources and in the fair and just distribution of the tax burden. Hadash works to ensure an economic policy that prevents the rise in the cost of living and fully compensates workers and the entire public for any such price increases.
  2. Ending the privatization of the public sector with an emphasis on sensitive social aspects: education, health, and welfare.
  3. Ensuring equal job opportunities for all sectors and establishing industrial and employment zones in all regions of the country. Ending all forms of discrimination in the labor market based on nationality and gender.
  4. Protecting workers' rights and striving for collective labor agreements, including agreements in the private sector, while ensuring a fair and dignified life for workers.
  5. Permanent increase of the minimum wage based on linking it to the rise in the cost of living, with an emphasis on the increase that most affects the poor and weak strata, and relative to the general official wage rate.
  6. Increasing welfare allowances relative to the rise in the cost of living, mainly unemployment and income support allowances, increasing old-age allowances - especially for those who do not receive a pension, and increasing child allowances.
  7. Permanent increase based on clear principles of allowances for people with special needs, linked to the cost of living and their living needs.
  8. Expanding the Public Health Law - both in terms of the basket of health services and the basket of medicines. Stopping the phenomenon of supplementary health insurance, which creates medicine for the poor and medicine for the rich.
  9. Hadash works to strengthen trade unions with the aim of protecting workers, a strengthening that includes all trade unions while striving for the General Organization of Workers (Histadrut) to be the address for workers in defending their rights in all sectors, including the private sector.
  10. Abolishing poverty-stricken neighborhoods, building residential buildings at the state's expense, and transferring them to the residents of these neighborhoods based on the principle of "home for a home." This while ensuring housing at a monthly rent adapted to young couples and families with many children.

C. Equality for the Arab Public

expand_more
  1. Recognition of the Arab-Palestinian public in Israel as a national minority in all fields - political, social, and cultural.
  2. Abolition of the policy of discrimination and national oppression in all areas.
  3. Absolute equality of rights for Arab citizens and the enactment of a Basic Law in this matter, stopping land confiscation and home demolitions, recognition of all existing Arab localities, and the establishment of elected local authorities in them.
  4. Granting rights to refugees in their homeland, the children of destroyed communities and the displaced since 1948.
  5. Equality in the basic budgets of Arab local authorities, including development, education, health, agriculture, industry, sport, and housing budgets, and the inclusion of Arab citizens in state institutions. Budgetary equality must take into account the social and economic conditions of the Arab public.
  6. Expanding the jurisdiction of Arab population centers in a way that ensures the absorption of population needs - in residential construction, establishment of industrial zones, institutions, and leisure and recreation facilities for society, culture, sport, and others.
  7. Ensuring the right of the Arab public to a curriculum that guarantees emphasis on their national identity and culture.
  8. A stubborn struggle against sectarian fragmentation among the Arab public, and the cancellation of mandatory conscription imposed on Arab and Druze youth.
  9. Liberation of Islamic endowments (Waqf) and respect for the right of Muslim citizens to establish their elected religious frameworks.
  10. Recognition of the representative bodies of the Arab public in Israel.
  11. Equality for Arab farmers.
  12. Ensuring equal employment opportunities for Arabs in the labor market and ending all forms of discrimination against them. Discrimination expressed in their participation rate in the labor market, especially towards women, but also in wage gaps.

D. Defense of Democratic Freedoms and Elimination of Racism

expand_more

Struggle against the threat of fascism, cancellation of all forms of religious coercion, and strictly ensuring freedom of speech.

  1. Adoption of a constitution for the state that guarantees and protects the basic rights of citizens.
  2. Repeal of the Israeli Nation-State Law approved by the Knesset on July 19, 2018.
  3. Struggle against racism and the dangers of fascism.
  4. Ensuring freedom of speech and the press, freedom of association, political action, and religious belief.
  5. Cancellation of emergency regulations.
  6. Cancellation of religious coercion laws and fighting all types of sectarian intolerance, whatever it may be.
  7. Against changing the system of proportional democratic elections to the Knesset.
  8. Cancellation of the mandatory conscription law.
  9. Recognition of the democratic right to refuse military service for reasons of conscience.

E. Guaranteeing Equal Rights for Women in All Spheres

expand_more
  1. Enactment of a Basic Law for women's equality rights based on the international document on the elimination of all forms of discrimination against women. Practical implementation of the Equal Employment Opportunities Law, the Women's Labor Law, and the Equal Pay for Equal Work Law.
  2. Application of the law against domestic violence and increased punishment.
  3. Significant increase in budgets allocated to assist women victims of violence and encouraging campaigns on this subject.
  4. Increasing women's representation and strengthening their status in all official and public institutions.

F. Action for Environmental Protection

expand_more
  1. To act for the allocation of sufficient budgets for the implementation of laws ensuring the containment and even prevention of pollution and dealing with the consequences of natural disasters. For the provision of sufficient and appropriate budgets to encourage the transition to green energy.
  2. Protection of water sources, seas, and rivers from pollution.
  3. Placement of reasonable and stricter systems for measuring pollution percentages.

G. Prohibition of Weapons of Mass Destruction

expand_more
  1. To act to prevent the proliferation and development of weapons of mass destruction.
  2. Pushing for the Israeli government to join the international treaty for the specific prevention of the proliferation and development of nuclear weapons and the cancellation of Israeli nuclear projects.
  3. Disarmament of weapons of mass destruction from the Middle East.

In order to achieve the aforementioned goals, Hadash works tirelessly and in full or partial, permanent or temporary cooperation with various factors, organizations, circles, and personalities based on an agreed program and out of mutual respect.

Hadash is a Jewish-Arab left-wing front advocating for an end to the occupation and the establishment of a just peace, civil, national and political equality, social justice and the advancement of workers' rights.
